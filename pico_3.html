The Rebouças Tunnel is located in the city and state of Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Linking the districts of Rio Comprido to Lagoa, this tunnel was designed by the Carlos Lacerda government with the aim of directly connecting the North and South zones of the then capital, avoiding the Center.

The tunnel was named in memory of the brothers André Rebouças and Antônio Rebouças, engineers from Bahia who had an outstanding role in road infrastructure and sanitation projects in Brazil during the Second Reign.

The Cosme Velho neighborhood has access to the tunnels through a set of lanes built between Largo do Boticário and Ladeira dos Guararapes. In length and volume, the Rebouças Tunnel is equivalent to half of the Mont Blanc Tunnel, the longest road tunnel at the time, with 11,600 meters in length. It was named in honor of the engineers and brothers André Rebouças and Antônio Rebouças.
