Santa Monica is a city located in the US state of California, in Los Angeles County. It is a coastal city in the west of the Los Angeles metropolitan area, situated on Santa Monica Bay and bordered by the Pacific Ocean. It was incorporated in 1886. Due to its pleasant climate, Santa Monica had become a tourist destination since before the 20th century, but it was from the 1980s onwards, with the revitalization of tourist attractions, that the city consolidated itself as a tourist and Employment Opportunities.

The Santa Monica Racecourse, where the famous carousel is a US National Historic Landmark. Located on the Santa Monica Pier, it was built in 1909. The "Majestic" is the oldest cinema in the city. Also known as the Mayfayr Theatre, built in the year 1912, it ceased to function in 1994 after the Northridge earthquake. The historic Route 66 ends in Santa Monica.

The National Register of Historic Places lists eight historic landmarks in Santa Monica, of which only one is a National Historic Landmark, the Santa Monica Looff Hippodrome. The first landmark was designated on April 11, 1977 and the most recent on June 26, 2019, the Bay Street Beach Historic District.
