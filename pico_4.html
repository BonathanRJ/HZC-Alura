Fundação Getulio Vargas (FGV) is a private Brazilian higher education institution, founded on December 20, 1944 with the initial objective of preparing qualified personnel for the country's public and private administration. It is headquartered in the city of Rio de Janeiro.

In 2016, it was considered one of the 10 best think tanks in the world, occupying the 9th place, and conquered the leadership in Latin America for the eighth consecutive year, according to the 2016 Global Go To Think Tank Index Report, produced by the University of Pennsylvania, in the United States.

And in 2020, it occupies the 3rd position among the most important think tanks in the world, increasing its leadership in Latin America for the twelfth consecutive year, according to the 2020 Global Go To Think Tank Index Report, in addition to being the 1st best think tank managed in the world for the 5th consecutive year, being top 10 in 10 categories evaluated, and to occupy other positions of excellence such as the 3rd best center for regional studies, 4th best for social policies, 4th in to watch and 5th in best conference in think tank . FGV also maintains more than 90 Study and Research Centers.
