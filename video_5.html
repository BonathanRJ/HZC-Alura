Nagoya or Nagoya is the largest city in the Chubu region of Japan. It is the capital and largest metropolitan region of Aichi Prefecture and the fourth most populous urban center in the country with 2,266,249 inhabitants. Situated between Tokyo to the east and Kyoto to the west, Nagoya is considered the largest economic center in central Japan, the city has one of the largest ports in Japan, contributing to its ranking as the third richest in the country, behind only from Tokyo and Osaka.

Nagoya's history dates back to 1610, when a great castle was erected by the Owari branch of the powerful Tokugawa shogunate. After the Meiji Restoration (1868), which marked the end of shogunal rule, Nagoya continued as a commercial center. The development of the port of Nagoya, especially after the Second World War.

Nagoya is north of Ise Bay on the Nōbi Plain. The city was built on low-level plateaus to ward off flood waters. The plain is one of the most fertile areas in the country. The Kiso River flows west along the city's border and the Shōnai River comes from the northeast and turns south towards the bay. The man-made Hori River was built as a canal in 1610. It flows from north to south as part of the Shonai River system.
